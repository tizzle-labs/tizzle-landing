Getting Started

What is Tizzle?

Tizzle is a decentralized event ticketing protocol built on the Solana blockchain. Instead of purchasing tickets in the traditional sense, attendees stake tokens to register for an event.

When you attend the event and your ticket is scanned at the venue, your staked tokens are unlocked and returned to your wallet after a short holding period. If you don't show up, the stake is forfeited.

This creates a real commitment mechanism that dramatically reduces no-shows. Attendees pay no platform fee — organizers pay a flat fee per capacity slot at creation time.

How do I sign in to Tizzle?

Tizzle uses Mobile Wallet Adapter (MWA) for authentication — there is no email, password, or username/password login. You sign in using your Solana wallet app.

  1. Open the Tizzle app and tap Connect Wallet on the welcome screen.
  2. Your installed wallet app (e.g. Phantom, Solflare) will open automatically via MWA.
  3. Approve the connection request in your wallet app.
  4. You are signed in. Your wallet public key is your identity on Tizzle.

If this is your first time, you will be taken through a short onboarding flow to set up your profile before entering the app.

What wallets are supported?

Any Solana wallet app that supports the Mobile Wallet Adapter (MWA) standard works with Tizzle. This includes:

  • Phantom
  • Solflare
  • Backpack
  • Any other MWA-compatible Solana wallet

You must have one of these wallet apps installed on the same device as the Tizzle app. Make sure your wallet has enough SOL to cover network transaction fees and any stake amounts.

What tokens can be used as stake?

Tizzle supports three token standards for event stakes:

  • SOL Native Solana — the most common stake token. All organizers can use SOL.
  • SPL Standard Solana Program Library tokens — fungible tokens like USDC, BONK, etc.
  • TOKEN-2022 Next-generation Solana token standard with extended functionality.

The organizer chooses the stake token and amount when creating an event. Attendees must have sufficient balance of that token to register.

What happens during onboarding?

The first time you connect your wallet, Tizzle walks you through a two-step onboarding:

  1. Set your username — choose a unique handle that identifies you on the platform. This can be changed later from your profile.
  2. Select your interests — pick categories of events you care about (music, sports, tech, art, etc.) to personalize your Explore feed.

Once onboarding is complete, you land on the main app with four tabs: Explore, Tickets, Create, and Scanner.

Organization

What is an organization?

An organization is your identity as an event organizer on Tizzle. It is the public-facing profile attached to your wallet that hosts events.

When attendees browse the Explore screen, they see events listed under their organizer's organization name. Your organization page can include a name, description, website, and social media links.

You must have an organization before you can create any events.

How do I create an organization?

Go to the Profile tab and tap Create Organization. Fill in the required details:

  • • Name — the public display name of your organization (required)
  • • Description — a short bio or mission statement for your org (required)
  • • Website — your organization's URL (optional)
  • • Social links — Twitter/X, Instagram, etc. (optional)

Creating an organization signs an on-chain transaction. After confirmation, your organization is live and linked to your wallet.

Can I have multiple organizations?

No. Each wallet address can be associated with exactly one organization. This is enforced at the protocol level.

If you need a second organization (e.g. for a different brand or entity), you would need to use a separate Solana wallet. Once you create an organization on a wallet, that slot is permanently taken.

How do I edit my organization?

Go to the Profile tab, tap your organization card, then tap Edit. You can update the name, description, website, and social links.

Saving edits submits an on-chain update transaction. The changes take effect once confirmed on Solana.

Creating Events

How do I create an event?

You must have an organization before creating events. Once you do, tap the Create tab (the + icon in the bottom navigation) and fill in the event form.

When you submit the form, a transaction is sent to the Solana network that:

  • Creates the event on-chain with all specified parameters
  • Deducts the platform fee from your wallet (0.002 SOL × capacity)
  • Makes the event immediately visible in the Explore tab

All event parameters are immutable once created — review everything carefully before confirming the transaction.

What fields are required to create an event?

The event creation form requires:

  • • Name — public event title
  • • Description — details about the event
  • • Location — venue name or address
  • • Start date & time — when the event begins
  • • End date & time — when the event ends (used to compute the unlock date)
  • • Capacity — maximum number of attendees (affects platform fee)
  • • Stake amount — tokens each attendee must lock to register (0 = free/no stake)
  • • Stake token — SOL, SPL token, or Token-2022
  • • Gatekeeper address — Solana wallet that can scan tickets (defaults to your wallet)
What is the platform fee and how is it calculated?

The platform fee is paid by the organizer when creating an event. The formula is:

Platform Fee = 0.0005 SOL × Capacity

For example, an event with 100 capacity costs 0.05 SOL to create. An event with 500 capacity costs 0.25 SOL.

This fee is deducted from your wallet at transaction time and is non-refundable. Attendees are never charged a platform fee — it is organizer-only.

What is the unlock date?

The unlock date is the earliest moment an attendee can claim their returned stake after attending. It is automatically calculated as:

Unlock Date = Event End Time + 7 days

This date is set at event creation time, is immutable, and is displayed on every event and ticket screen. The 7-day holding period is enforced on-chain by the Tizzle program.

After the unlock date passes, attendees who were successfully scanned in can claim their full stake back from the Tickets tab.

What is the gatekeeper address?

The gatekeeper is the Solana wallet address that has permission to scan and validate tickets for your event. Only this wallet's device can use the Scanner tab to check in attendees.

By default, the gatekeeper is set to your own wallet address (the event organizer). If you want a different person or device to scan tickets — for example, a venue staff member — enter their Solana wallet address in the gatekeeper field.

The gatekeeper address is set at event creation and cannot be changed after the event is created.

Can I edit or cancel an event after creating it?

Event parameters are stored immutably on the Solana blockchain. Core details like capacity, stake amount, unlock date, and gatekeeper address cannot be changed after creation.

Review all fields carefully before submitting the creation transaction. If an event needs to be cancelled, all registered attendees who haven't been scanned will be eligible for a refund of their stake. Contact the Tizzle team via @tizzleai for assistance with cancellations.

Attending Events

How do I find events to attend?

Open the Explore tab (the first tab in the bottom navigation). It shows all upcoming public events on the Tizzle protocol. You can browse by category or scroll through the full list.

Tap any event card to see full details including name, organizer, location, date/time, capacity, stake amount, stake token, and unlock date.

How do I register for an event?

On the event detail screen, tap Register. The app will show you a confirmation screen with the stake amount and token that will be locked.

  1. Review the stake amount and tap Confirm.
  2. Your wallet app opens via MWA to sign the transaction.
  3. Once the transaction is confirmed, your ticket is created on-chain.
  4. The ticket appears in your Tickets tab with status VALID.

Make sure your wallet has sufficient balance of the stake token plus SOL for transaction fees before registering.

How do I show my ticket at the event?

Go to your Tickets tab, find the event, and tap the ticket to open it. The ticket screen shows a QR code that the gatekeeper scans at the venue entrance.

Keep your phone screen on and bright when presenting the QR code. The gatekeeper uses the Tizzle Scanner to read it and confirm your attendance on-chain.

What are the ticket statuses?

Your ticket can be in one of these states:

  • VALID Registered and ready. Your stake is locked in escrow. Show this QR code at the event.
  • USED You attended and were scanned in. Your stake is pending return — wait for the unlock date to claim.
  • CLAIMABLE The unlock date has passed. Go to the Tickets tab and tap Claim to receive your stake back.
  • REFUNDED You successfully claimed your stake. The tokens are back in your wallet.
  • NO-SHOW You registered but were never scanned in. Your stake is forfeited and cannot be recovered.
  • CANCELLED The event was cancelled. Your stake is automatically refunded.
Where do I view all my tickets?

Open the Tickets tab (the second icon in the bottom navigation). It shows all tickets associated with your connected wallet — past, present, upcoming, and claimable. Tap any ticket to view its QR code, event details, and current status.

Gatekeeper

Who is the gatekeeper?

The gatekeeper is the Solana wallet address authorized to scan and validate tickets for a specific event. Only the wallet that matches the gatekeeper address set at event creation can use the Scanner tab to check in attendees.

By default this is the organizer's own wallet, but a separate address can be specified at creation — for example, a venue staff member's wallet. The gatekeeper address is fixed after creation and cannot be reassigned.

How do I scan a ticket?

Open the Scanner tab (the fourth icon in the bottom navigation) on the device connected to the gatekeeper wallet. Point the camera at the attendee's QR code.

  1. The scanner automatically detects the QR code.
  2. The ticket is validated on-chain via a Solana transaction.
  3. A result screen appears: green checkmark for valid, or an error message for invalid tickets.
  4. After 3 seconds, the scanner resets and is ready for the next scan.

There is a 3-second cooldown between scans to prevent duplicate submissions.

What do the scan results mean?
  • VALID Ticket accepted. Attendee is checked in and their stake is now pending return.
  • ALREADY USED This ticket has already been scanned. Deny entry.
  • WRONG EVENT The ticket is for a different event. Deny entry.
  • INVALID The QR code is not a valid Tizzle ticket. Deny entry.
Why isn't the Scanner tab visible?

The Scanner tab is always visible in the bottom navigation. However, scanning will only succeed when your connected wallet matches the gatekeeper address for the event being scanned.

If you receive an "unauthorized" error when scanning, the wallet connected to Tizzle does not match the event's gatekeeper address. Connect the correct wallet or contact the event organizer.

Staking & Refunds

How does the stake-to-attend protocol work?

When you register for an event, the specified stake amount is locked in an on-chain escrow vault controlled by the Tizzle program — not by the organizer or any third party.

  1. Register — stake is locked in escrow. Ticket status: VALID.
  2. Attend & get scanned — ticket marked USED. Stake is now claimable after the unlock date.
  3. Unlock date passes — ticket becomes CLAIMABLE.
  4. Claim — stake returned to your wallet. Ticket status: REFUNDED.

If you don't attend, the ticket moves to NO-SHOW after the event and the stake is permanently forfeited.

What happens if I don't attend the event?

If your ticket is not scanned before the event ends, your ticket status changes to NO-SHOW. Your staked tokens are permanently forfeited — they cannot be recovered.

This is the core commitment mechanism of Tizzle. If you know you won't attend, contact the organizer as soon as possible — event cancellation policies are set by the organizer, not the protocol.

Is there a minimum stake amount?

Yes. The Tizzle program enforces that stake_amount must be greater than 0. Free (zero-stake) events are not supported at the protocol level — every event requires attendees to lock a token amount as commitment.

Organizers can set any non-zero amount that makes sense for their event — even a very small stake is enough to create the commitment mechanic and reduce no-shows.

How do I claim my stake back?

Once your ticket status is CLAIMABLE (unlock date has passed and you attended):

  1. Go to the Tickets tab.
  2. Tap the ticket with CLAIMABLE status.
  3. Tap Claim and approve the transaction in your wallet app.
  4. Your staked tokens are transferred back to your wallet. Ticket status changes to REFUNDED.

Claims require a small SOL transaction fee. There is no deadline to claim — your tokens stay in the escrow vault until you initiate the claim.

Why is there a 7-day waiting period before I can claim?

The 7-day holding period (unlock date = event end + 7 days) is a protocol-enforced settlement window. It gives time for:

  • Dispute resolution if there are scanning discrepancies
  • On-chain finality and indexing
  • Ensuring all scan transactions have settled before funds move

This period is set at event creation and is immutable. It applies uniformly to all attendees of a given event.

Profile & Settings

What is shown on my profile?

The Profile tab displays your Tizzle identity:

  • • Avatar — a letter avatar generated from your username initial
  • • Username & display name
  • • Wallet address — your connected Solana public key (truncated)
  • • Stats — total events attended and events organized
  • • Badges — achievement badges earned from activity on Tizzle
  • • Organization — your linked organization card (if you have one)
How do I edit my profile?

Tap the Edit button on the Profile tab. You can update:

  • • Username (must be unique)
  • • Display name
  • • Interests / event categories

Profile updates are saved to the Tizzle backend and do not require an on-chain transaction.

How do I disconnect my wallet?

Go to the Profile tab and scroll to the bottom. Tap Disconnect Wallet. This logs you out of Tizzle and returns you to the welcome screen.

Disconnecting does not delete your profile or affect any on-chain data — your tickets, organization, and events remain intact. You can reconnect the same wallet at any time to resume where you left off.

Troubleshooting

My transaction failed — what should I do?

Most transaction failures are caused by one of these:

  • 1. Insufficient SOL for gas — Every Solana transaction requires a small SOL fee (~0.000005 SOL). Make sure your wallet has SOL even if you're staking a different token.
  • 2. Insufficient stake token balance — Confirm you have enough of the required stake token in your wallet.
  • 3. Insufficient SOL for platform fee (organizers) — Creating an event requires 0.002 SOL × capacity in your wallet.
  • 4. Network congestion — Retry the transaction. Solana occasionally experiences brief congestion.

Failed transactions do not deduct your stake or fee. It is safe to retry.

The scanner isn't working — what should I do?

Check the following:

  • • Camera permission — Go to your phone's Settings and ensure Tizzle has camera access enabled.
  • • 3-second cooldown — The scanner has a mandatory 3-second reset between scans. Wait for the camera viewfinder to reappear.
  • • Gatekeeper mismatch — Confirm your connected wallet matches the gatekeeper address for this event.
  • • Lighting — Ensure the QR code is well-lit and not glare-obscured. Ask the attendee to increase screen brightness.
My ticket isn't showing as CLAIMABLE — why?

A ticket only becomes CLAIMABLE when both conditions are met:

  1. Your ticket was scanned and confirmed (status moved to USED)
  2. The unlock date has passed (event end + 7 days)

If your ticket still shows USED, the unlock date has not yet arrived. Check the event details for the exact unlock date and time.

If you attended but your ticket still shows VALID (never moved to USED), your QR code may not have been successfully scanned. Contact the event organizer or reach out to @tizzleai.
